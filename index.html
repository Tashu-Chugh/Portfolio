The human face is a very complex and distinct part of the body. In fact, it is one of the most complex signaling systems available to us; It includes more than 40 muscles that are structurally and functionally independent, so that each of them can operate independently of the others. It is one of the strongest indicators of emotions.

Oh yeah, that's good. See for yourself.

When we laugh or cry, we display our emotions, allowing others to peek into our minds as they "read" our faces based on changes in key facial components, such as the eyes, eyebrows, eyelids, nose, and lips.

And finally, this. Checkmate.

All the muscles in our bodies are supported by nerves that connect from all parts of the body to the spinal cord and brain. This nerve communication is bidirectional, meaning that the nerve causes muscle contractions based on brain signals, and at the same time sends information about the condition of the muscles to the brain.
